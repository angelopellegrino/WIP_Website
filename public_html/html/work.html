I started a career in technology at the age of 15 under the guidance of my father's friend Al. He had brought me aboard to his company Total Computer Group, where I was exposed to quality assurance. This was the first step in the foundation of my work ethic. Quality assurance taught me many things. For one, I learned how to look for problems and how to report them. You had to take steps to break the program and potentially use it in ways that it was not designed for. after you found a problem, it was important to document the steps taken to recreate the problem. At the time I had no programming experience so there would be no actual debugging done by me. In the end, I found well over 100 bugs.


My next major breakthrough was with the I.T. Department at NIF Group Inc, where I was taken under their wing in the summer of 2012. At first they had assigned me small tasks, such as updating images on a website and working on excel spreadsheets. Eventually they presented me with bigger projects to handle. Their NYC office was moving into a new building and I was given the task to find a new ISP and new networking equipment for the new location. Working at NIF taught me that things needed to be completed with high accuracy, and in an orderly and timely manner. Most importantly I learned that documentation was key.
